Frequently Asked Questions

What type of router/modem do I need for BoibyLink service?

For our Full Fibre (FTTP) plans, we provide a professional-grade WiFi 6 router that's optimized for high-speed connections. For Part Fibre (FTTN/FTTC) plans, we offer a compatible modem-router combination device. Both are included at no extra cost with your plan.

What's the difference between WiFi 5, WiFi 6, and WiFi 6E?

WiFi 5 (802.11ac) is the previous generation standard, offering good speeds for most households. WiFi 6 (802.11ax) provides faster speeds, better performance in crowded areas, and improved battery life for devices. WiFi 6E extends WiFi 6 to include the 6 GHz band for even less congestion and higher performance. Our highest tier plans include WiFi 6E routers to maximize your internet experience.

What is the difference between Full Fibre and Part Fibre?

Full Fibre (FTTP) means fiber optic cables run directly to your premises, offering the fastest and most reliable speeds with symmetrical upload and download capabilities.

Part Fibre (FTTN/FTTC) means fiber runs to a node or cabinet in your neighborhood, with the final connection using existing copper lines. This provides good speeds but may be affected by distance from the node and doesn't offer symmetrical upload speeds.

Do you offer WiFi 7 compatible equipment?

Yes. We now offer WiFi 7 routers with our 2000/2000 Mbps plan. WiFi 7 provides incredible speeds, lower latency, and better handling of multiple devices simultaneously. It's the latest standard in wireless technology and future-proofs your home network.

What is Ethernet and how does it compare to WiFi?

Ethernet is a wired networking technology that uses cables to connect devices to your network. Compared to WiFi:

  • Ethernet provides more stable, reliable connections with lower latency
  • Ethernet typically offers faster speeds as it's not subject to wireless interference
  • WiFi provides mobility and convenience without cables
  • WiFi is more susceptible to interference from walls, appliances, and other networks

For activities requiring maximum stability like gaming, video conferencing, or large file transfers, Ethernet is generally superior.

What Ethernet port speeds do devices typically support?

Ethernet port speeds vary by device age and quality:

  • Fast Ethernet (10/100 Mbps): Found on older devices, limited to 100 Mbps
  • Gigabit Ethernet (10/100/1000 Mbps): Common on most devices from the last decade, supports up to 1000 Mbps
  • 2.5 Gigabit Ethernet: Becoming more common on premium devices and motherboards
  • 5 Gigabit Ethernet: Found on high-end workstations and networking equipment
  • 10 Gigabit Ethernet: Available on professional-grade equipment and high-end PCs

To take full advantage of our faster plans (500 Mbps and above), you'll need at least a Gigabit Ethernet port on your device.

Do Ethernet cable categories affect speed?

Yes, Ethernet cable categories significantly impact maximum supported speeds:

  • Cat 5: Up to 100 Mbps (outdated)
  • Cat 5e: Up to 1000 Mbps (1 Gbps)
  • Cat 6: Up to 10 Gbps (at shorter distances)
  • Cat 6a: Up to 10 Gbps (at longer distances)
  • Cat 7/8: Up to 40 Gbps (for professional use)

For our gigabit and multi-gigabit plans, we recommend at least Cat 5e cables, though Cat 6 or higher is ideal for future-proofing.

Will my device support your highest speed plans?

Device support for high-speed internet depends on several factors:

  • Device age: Older devices (pre-2015) may have hardware limitations
  • Network interface: WiFi capabilities and Ethernet port speeds
  • Premium vs. budget devices: Premium devices typically have better networking hardware

As a general guide:

  • Most devices from the last 5 years can handle up to 500 Mbps on WiFi
  • Premium devices from the last 3-5 years can typically handle gigabit speeds on WiFi
  • For multi-gigabit speeds (2000 Mbps), you'll need recent high-end devices with 2.5G+ Ethernet or WiFi 6/6E/7
  • You will likely be able to get better, more stable speeds on ethernet if you have a desktop computer or older laptop.

We can help assess your devices during installation to ensure compatibility with your chosen plan.

Can I use my own modem/router with your service?

Yes, you can use your own equipment. You are not locked into our provided routers. However if you use your own router, we may not be able to provide full technical support for connectivity issues, but we will try our best.
